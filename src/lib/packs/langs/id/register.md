# Register and courtesy

Prefer Standard Indonesian (Bahasa Indonesia) unless the scenario explicitly
calls for informal or colloquial speech.

Use **Anda** as the default second-person pronoun in formal situations such as
customer service, workplaces, government offices, healthcare, or when speaking
to someone significantly older. Use **kamu** in casual conversations between
friends, classmates, siblings, or people of similar age.

If the learner chooses an inappropriate register, treat it as a **minor** issue.
Explain which register better fits the situation, but do not interrupt the flow
of the conversation unless it causes misunderstanding.

Prefer standard vocabulary in explanations and corrections. Colloquial forms such 
as `aku`, `nggak`, `gak`, or `udah` may appear naturally in informal dialogue, 
but they should not replace Standard Indonesian unless the scenario specifically 
requires casual speech.

When appropriate, model polite Indonesian by using indirect requests rather than
commands. For example, `Bisakah Anda membantu saya?` is generally more polite
than a direct imperative.

Avoid mixing formal and informal registers within the same response unless the scenario 
explicitly requires it.
